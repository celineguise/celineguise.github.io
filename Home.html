The Affordable Care Act and Gig Employment (working paper)

Abstract:
The gig economy allows consumers to purchase goods and services with ease, and allows workers to provide goods and services to consumers, on their own schedules. The Affordable Care Act extended the age at which younger individuals can remain on their parent’s insurance from 19 to 26 and allowed individuals to purchase their own health insurance. This paper looks at the impact of the Affordable Care Act on a younger individual’s probability to partake in gig work, using Uber as a proxy. I find that younger individuals are 0.04% less likely to work in the gig economy, post Uber deployment.
